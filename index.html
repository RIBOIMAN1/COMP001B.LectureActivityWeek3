About Halloween

Halloween, celebrated annually on October 31st, is a festival that has its roots in ancient Celtic traditions. The Celts celebrated Samhain, a festival marking the end of the harvest season and the beginning of winter. They believed that on this night, the boundary between the living and the dead was blurred, allowing spirits to roam the earth. People would light bonfires and wear costumes to ward off these wandering spirits, a tradition that has evolved into the modern-day practice of donning spooky costumes and masks.

More About Halloween

In contemporary times, Halloween has become a beloved holiday characterized by an array of festive activities. Children and adults alike indulge in the fun of trick-or-treating, where they dress up in costumes and go door-to-door collecting candy from neighbors. The thrill of haunted houses, ghost stories, and horror movies add to the excitement, creating an atmosphere of both fun and fright. Pumpkin carving is another cherished tradition, with people transforming pumpkins into eerie jack-o'-lanterns to decorate their homes.

Even More About Halloween

Aside from the playful aspects, Halloween also provides a time for communities to come together and celebrate. Parties and parades often feature elaborate costumes and decorations, showcasing the creativity and effort that people put into their Halloween celebrations. Schools and local organizations frequently host events that include games, contests, and themed activities, ensuring that everyone can partake in the spooky festivities. In many ways, Halloween serves as a cultural touchstone that brings people together, allowing them to revel in the eerie and the extraordinary.

Fun Facts About Halloween:

  • Jack-o'-lanterns originated in Ireland and were originally carved from turnips or potatoes. When Irish immigrants brought the tradition to America, they found that pumpkins were easier to carve.
  • The word "witch" comes from the Old English word "wicce," meaning "wise woman." Witches were highly respected figures back in the day.
  • The largest Halloween parade in the US is the Village Halloween Parade in New York City. It attracts over 2 million spectators and features more than 50,000 costumed participants.
